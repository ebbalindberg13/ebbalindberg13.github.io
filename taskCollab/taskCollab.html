Rentico

Overview
TaskCollab is an app for hybrid remote collaboration with a backlog feature. This was part of my Graphical Interface course where the task was to create a video conferencing app that supported hybrid meetings. There are a lot of problems when having hybrid meetings such as audio feedback or unsynchronized sound if people are connected with multiple devices in the meeting room. Continue reading to see how we solved the problem!


When
Autumn 2021
Team members
Geoffrey Chen, Norea Sandgren,
Maja Ulen, Panpan Zhang
Methods
Sketching, wireframing, prototyping

Requirements

  • Video and audio calls with breakout rooms
  • Personal and group messages
  • Hybrid situation meetings
    When some people sit in the same room, and others are joined from elsewhere.
  • Live collaborative creation
    We chose to have a backlog feature that could be used during meetings.

Wireframes

Time was spent on the ideation and wireframing to explore different user flows. The two areas that we focused on the most were how to join a hybrid meeting and how to reach and use the to-do list during meetings.

Result

Join a hybrid meeting

  1. Project page
    Here all the tasks within the project can be seen. Swipe right to see other projects.
  2. Task room
    The details about the tasks can be seen and edited here. Meeting for all the people working on the task can be joined and created here.
  3. Join hybrid meeting
    With the help of the GPS location, the app interferes if you are close to another person in the meeting and when choosing that option, the settings for mics and speakers are adjusted to prevent audio feedback.
  4. Video meeting
    See the description below for details on the video conference settings.

Meeting

An explanatory overlay pops up when the user enters a meeting for the first time. 6 participants in the meeting are displayed on the screen, simply swipe for more people. To access the task page the users have three options. There is a simplified task page in the meeting that can be accessed by either swiping to the first page of the meeting or using the more options menu. The third option is to minimize the meeting view to reveal the task page with all details. Standard video conferencing settings can be found at the bottom of the page.

Reflection

We were a group of 5 people working on this project. When we went from wireframing to designing the hi-fi prototype, it became noticeable that we all had different styles when designing. Some preferred having larger text and bigger components, while others kept it smaller. People also had different ways of grouping items. Halfway through designing we had to create some sort of guidelines regarding the sizing and spacing of elements. Designing a coherent interface together with other people is something you'd do in real life, and I think this project taught me a lot about that!
